Liberty Tattoo Zine

Home Work Play About

In this project, I designed a zine based on a personal “collection” developed earlier in the semester, which was later distributed in the library. Throughout the term, we were asked to select a topic to collect; I chose tattoos. I gathered over 200 tattoo images from people I know, individuals I encountered in public, and online sources. Using this collection, I developed the concept and visual direction for the zine, incorporating the imagery throughout the design. The zine is inspired by the tattoo studio I visit in Atlanta and is designed to visually reflect the atmosphere of that space. The narrative places the reader as the main character, visiting the studio to receive a mystery tattoo from a gumball machine. After receiving the tattoo, the final page provides aftercare instructions. There are four different versions of the zine, and the mystery tattoo the reader receives depends on which edition they get.
